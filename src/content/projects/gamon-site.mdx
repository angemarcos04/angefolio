## Overview

Gamon Site is a digital garden for notes, project logs, longer writing, and small pieces of personal software. It is meant to grow gradually instead of behaving like a polished publication from day one.

## Problem

Project context and useful notes are easy to lose when they live across temporary documents, social posts, and repositories. A personal archive needs enough structure to stay navigable without making every entry feel formal.

## Approach

The site uses Astro content collections and MDX as the durable content layer. Pagefind is planned for static search, while project logs and topic-based navigation will connect related work over time.

## Current status

The information architecture and content system are in progress. Search, archive navigation, and the long-term publishing workflow still need to be completed and tested with real content.

## What I am learning

- Designing content types that can evolve without a database
- Balancing a structured archive with low-friction writing
- Treating navigation and search as part of the content model
