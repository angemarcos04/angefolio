## Context

I am curious about AI personas as a combination of interface writing, constraints, and repeatable behavior. The experiment is deliberately separate from my main project work: it is a place to explore tone and interaction design, not a replacement for real people or expertise.

## What I tried

So far, the work is mostly structured notes. I am outlining narrow persona purposes, allowed behaviors, refusal boundaries, and small prompt sets that can reveal when a voice becomes inconsistent or misleading.

One direction is a workshop companion that asks useful debugging questions. Another is a fictional writing voice for creative exercises. Neither is a finished application.

## Current result

The concepts are still ideas. The strongest observation is that decorative personality is easy to write, while dependable boundaries and honest capability statements require much more care.

There is no public demo, trained model, or autonomous agent attached to this entry.

## Notes

- Personas should clearly identify that they are software.
- A defined task is more useful than a large fictional backstory.
- Test prompts should include ambiguity, correction, and requests outside the intended scope.
- Sensitive, medical, legal, or high-stakes roles are outside this experiment.
- Any future prototype should avoid collecting personal conversation data by default.

## Next steps

If the idea moves beyond notes, the next step is a small offline prompt evaluation set with explicit success and failure criteria. If that does not reveal a useful interaction pattern, the concept can remain archived as a writing exercise.
